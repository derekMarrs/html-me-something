One of my favorite hobbys right now has to be rock climbing. It is such a blast and everytime you finish you are super sore because it works out so many muscles. However it does get pretty nerve wrecking at times. The reason for this is probably pretty obvious, but you are pretty high at times. And the only thing that is keeping you from falling to your death is this little half inch rope that another person has to hold to keep you up. To me that isn't the worst part though. On top rope I am never really scared. This is because if you miss a hold or slip, you don't really fall at all. The person belaying you holds onto the rope so you just hang there. However, with lead climbing, if you fall or slip, you are usually falling around 6 feet before the rope catches you. That doesn't sound like a lot but trust me! It is. That is why I really only boulder now, because the highest you get it a about 15 feet. You don't even need a rope to boulder!
